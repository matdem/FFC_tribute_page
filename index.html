Aaron Swartz

"Information is power. But like all power, there are those who want to keep it for themselves."

-- Aaron Swartz

Aaron Hillel Swartz was an American computer programmer, entrepreneur, writer, political organizer, and Internet hacktivist. He was involved in the development of the web feed format RSS, the Markdown publishing format, the organization Creative Commons, and the website framework web.py, and was a co-founder of the social news site Reddit.

  • 1986 - Born of Aaron Swartz
  • 1999 - At 13 years old, he created the web site Theinfo.org.
  • 2000 - He joined the working group that authored the RSS 1.0 web syndication specification.
  • 2001 - He joined the RDFCore working group at the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) to on RDF/XML specifications.
  • 2004 - He was a major contributor to John Gruber's Markdown and author of its html2text translator.
  • 2005 - Became a co-founder of Reddit by mergering a working project, Infogami, and by rewritting the codebase.
  • 2007 - Launch Jottit with Infogami co-founder Simon Carstensen, a firm attempt to create another markdown driven content management system in Python.
  • 2008 - Start activism and founded Watchdog.net to aggregate and visualize data about politicians. In the same year, he wrote the Guerrilla Open Access Manifesto.
  • Still 2008 - Swartz downloaded about 2.7 million federal court documents stored in the PACER database to make them free to public.
  • Always in 2008 - He worked with Virgil Griffith to design ans implement Tor2Web, an HTTP proxy for Tor-hidden services.
  • 2009 - Swartz led the first activism event of his career with the Progressive Change Campaign Committee.
  • 2010 - He co-founded Demand Progress a political advocacy group that organizes people online to take action by contacting Congress and other leaders.
  • In late 2010 and early 2011 - He used his acces as a student to download a large number of academic journal articles through MIT's computer network from the JSTOR, a digital repository.
  • 2011 - Swartz was arrested by the MIT police. Few months later, he was indicated by a federal grand jury on charges of wire fraud, computer fraud, unlawfully obtaining information form a protected computer, and recklessy damaging a protected computer.
  • 2011-2012 - With Kevin Poulsen and James Dolan, they designed and implemented DeadDrop, a system that allows anonymous informants to send electronic documents whithout fear of disclosure.
  • 2012 - Swartz was involved in the campaign to prevent passage of the Stop Online Piracy Act (SOPA). At the same time, federal prosecutors filed a superdeding indictment adding nine more felony counts, which increased Swartz,s maximum exposure to 50 years of imprisonment ansd $1 million in fines.
  • January 2013 - Swartz died of suicide in his Brooklyn apartment at the age of 26.
  • Later in 2013 - Aaron's Law is introduced to exclude terms of service violations from the 1986 Computer Fraud and Abuse Act from the wire fraud statute.
